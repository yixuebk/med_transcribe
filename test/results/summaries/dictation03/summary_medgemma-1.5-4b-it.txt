Subjective:
77 year old female presenting with progressive dyspnea over five days, orthopnea (requiring three pillows), paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnea (occurring nightly for the past three evenings), bilateral lower extremity edema extending to mid-shin, and a weight gain of 4.5 kg in one week. Denies chest pain, palpitations, syncope, fever, productive cough, or recent travel. History includes chronic systolic heart failure with reduced ejection fraction (EF 35%), persistent atrial fibrillation (AF), hypertension, and chronic kidney disease stage 3b. Medications include furosemide 40 mg daily, metaprolol succinate 100 mg daily, lisinopril 10 mg daily, and apixaban 5 mg twice daily.

Objective:
Vital signs: BP 168/92 mmHg, HR 112 bpm (irregularly irregular), RR 22 breaths/min, SpO2 90% on room air, Temp 36.8°C. Physical exam: Mild respiratory distress, neck vein distension (JVD) of 12 cm above sternal angle at 45 degrees, tachycardia with irregularly irregular rhythm, S1 and S2 present, S3 gallop noted, no murmurs. Respiratory exam: Bibasilar crackles to mid-lung fields bilaterally. Abdomen: Soft, mildly distended, positive hepatojugular reflux. Extremities: Bilateral 2+ pitting edema to mid-tibia. Neurologic: Alert and oriented x3x3, no focal deficits. ECG: Atrial fibrillation with rapid ventricular response (RVR) at 115 bpm. Chest radiograph: Cardiomegaly, bilateral interstitial edema, small pleural effusions. Labs: BNP 1240 pg/mL, Troponin mildly elevated (28 ng/L), Creatinine 1.9 mg/dL (baseline 1.6), BUN 42 mg/dL, Sodium 131 mEq/L, Potassium 4.8 mEq/L, Hemoglobin 11.2 g/dL.

Assessment:
77 year old female with known heart failure with reduced ejection fraction presenting with acute decompensated congestive heart failure (ADHF). Likely precipitated by uncontrolled hypertension and atrial fibrillation with rapid ventricular response. Evidence of significant volume overload including pulmonary edema, JVD, peripheral edema, and weight gain. Elevated BNP supports diagnosis of ADHF. Worsening renal function noted (Creatinine 1.9 mg/dL from baseline 1.6 mg/dL).

Plan:
Admit to telemetry. Initiate intravenous furosemide 80 mg. Begin rate control with intravenous diltiazem infusion, titrate to target HR < 100 bpm. Implement fluid restriction (1.5 L/day) and sodium restriction (< 2 g/day). Monitor urine output closely and daily weights. Cardiology consultation requested for further management and optimization of guideline-directed medical therapy.
