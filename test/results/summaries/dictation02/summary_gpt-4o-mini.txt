Subjective:
Michael Johnson is a 40-year-old male presenting with acute lower back pain that began three days ago after lifting a heavy box. The pain is sharp, localized to the right lower lumbar area, rated 7 out of 10, exacerbated by bending and prolonged sitting. He has attempted over-the-counter ibuprofen with partial relief. Denies radiation, numbness, tingling, weakness, bowel or bladder incontinence, and saddle anesthesia.

Objective:
Vital signs: BP 126/78, HR 72, RR 14, Temp 98.4 °F. Patient appears mildly uncomfortable but in no acute distress. Physical examination reveals tenderness to palpation over the right lumbar paraspinal muscles, with no midline spinal tenderness. Neurologic examination shows strength 5/5 in bilateral lower extremities, intact sensation, symmetric reflexes 2+, and negative straight leg raise bilaterally. Gait is normal.

Assessment:
Acute mechanical low back strain without red flag symptoms.

Plan:
Conservative management including rest, activity modification, and non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs as needed. Initiate physical therapy exercises. Advised on proper lifting techniques. Return precautions discussed (worsening pain, new neurologic symptoms, bowel or bladder dysfunction). Follow up in two weeks if not improving.
