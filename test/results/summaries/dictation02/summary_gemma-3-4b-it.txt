Subjective:
40-year-old male presenting with acute onset of lower back pain three days ago following lifting a heavy box while moving furniture. Patient describes the pain as sharp and localized to the right lower lumbar region, rated 7/10, exacerbated by bending and prolonged sitting. He denies radiation, numbness, tingling, weakness, bowel or bladder dysfunction. He has tried over-the-counter ibuprofen with partial relief. No chronic medical conditions reported. Appendectomy in 2010. Medications: Ibuprofen as needed. Allergies: NKDA.

Objective:
Vital Signs: BP 126/78, HR 72, RR 14, Temp 98.4°F. General: Patient appears mildly uncomfortable but in no acute distress. Back: Tenderness to palpation over the right lumbar paraspinal muscles. No midline spinal tenderness. Neurologic: Strength 5/5 bilaterally in lower extremities, intact sensation, reflexes 2+ and symmetric, negative straight leg raise bilaterally. Gait normal.

Assessment:
Acute mechanical low back strain without red flag symptoms.

Plan:
Recommend conservative management including rest, activity modification, NSAIDs as needed, and initiation of physical therapy exercises. Advised patient on proper lifting techniques. Return precautions discussed: worsening pain, development of neurologic symptoms or bowel/bladder dysfunction. Follow up in two weeks if not improving.
