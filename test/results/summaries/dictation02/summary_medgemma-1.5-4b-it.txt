Subjective:
40-year-old male presenting with acute onset lower back pain for three days following lifting a heavy box. Pain is localized to the right lower lumbar region, described as sharp, rated 7/10, and exacerbated by bending and prolonged sitting. No radiation, numbness, tingling, weakness, bowel or bladder dysfunction reported. Tried OTC ibuprofen with partial relief. Denies fever, weight loss, recent trauma beyond lifting injury, or neurologic deficits.

Objective:
Vital signs: BP 126/78, HR 72, RR 14, Temp 98.4 F. General: Mildly uncomfortable. Back: Tenderness over right lumbar paraspinal muscles. No midline spinal tenderness. Neurologic: Strength 5/5 bilaterally, sensation intact, reflexes 2+ symmetric, negative straight leg raise bilaterally. Gait normal.

Assessment:
Acute mechanical low back strain without red flag symptoms in a 40-year-old male.

Plan:
Recommend conservative management including rest, activity modification, NSAIDs as needed, and initiation of physical therapy exercises. Advised on proper lifting techniques. Return precautions discussed: worsening pain, development of neurologic symptoms or bowel/bladder dysfunction. Follow up in two weeks if not improving.
