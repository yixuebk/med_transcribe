Subjective:
Patient presents with acute onset of lower back pain three days ago after lifting a heavy box while moving furniture. Pain is described as sharp and localized to the right lower lumbar region, rated 7/10. Pain worsens with bending and prolonged sitting. Denies radiation down the legs, numbness, tingling, weakness, bowel or bladder incontinence or saddle anesthesia. Tried over-the-counter ibuprofen with partial relief.

Objective:
Vital signs: BP 126/78, HR 72, RR 14, Temp 98.4 F. General: Patient appears mildly uncomfortable but in no acute distress. Back: Tenderness to palpation over the right lumbar paraspinal muscles. No midline spinal tenderness. Neurologic: Strength 5/5 bilaterally in lower extremities, sensation intact, reflexes 2+ and symmetric, negative straight leg raise bilaterally. Gait normal.

Assessment:
40-year-old male with acute mechanical low back strain without red flag symptoms.

Plan:
Recommend conservative management including rest, activity modification, non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs as needed, and initiation of physical therapy exercises. Advised patient on proper lifting techniques. Return precautions discussed including worsening pain, development of neurologic symptoms or bowel/bladder dysfunction. Follow up in two weeks if not improving.
