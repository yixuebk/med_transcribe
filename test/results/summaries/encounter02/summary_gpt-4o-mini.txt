Subjective:
The patient reports intermittent substernal chest discomfort over the past three to four months, triggered by exertion and occasionally occurring at rest. The discomfort radiates to the left shoulder, medial left arm, and sometimes into the jaw, lasting 5-10 minutes and resolving with rest or sublingual nitroglycerin. Reports dyspnea on exertion, diaphoresis, mild nausea, and episodes of near syncope without losing consciousness. In the past month, also experiences orthopnea, needing two pillows to sleep, and several episodes of sudden awakening gasping for air. Notes bilateral ankle swelling and recent weight gain of 8 pounds over two months.

Objective:
Vital signs: Blood pressure 152/94 mmHg, heart rate 92 bpm with slight irregularity. Physical examination reveals an S3 gallop, soft systolic murmur at the apex, bibasilar crackles, and 1+ pitting edema in both ankles.

Assessment:
Concerns for obstructive coronary artery disease with potential ischemic cardiomyopathy and early congestive heart failure, considering patient's risk factors including long-standing hypertension, type 2 diabetes, history of smoking, and family history of cardiovascular disease.

Plan:
1. Order a 12-lead electrocardiogram to evaluate for ischemic changes or arrhythmias. 2. Laboratory studies: lipid panel, comprehensive metabolic panel, BNP, and repeat hemoglobin A1C. 3. Transthoracic echocardiogram to assess left ventricular ejection fraction and regional wall motion abnormalities. 4. Stress test with potential nuclear imaging given pretest probability for coronary artery disease. 5. Depending on results, consider cardiac catheterization for further assessment. 6. Optimize medical therapy: increase ACE inhibitor, possibly switch to angiotensin receptor neprilysin inhibitor, start a beta-blocker, add loop diuretic, ensure high-intensity statin, and consider an SGLT2 inhibitor. 7. Dietary sodium restriction to under 2 grams daily and daily weight monitoring. 8. Advise to seek emergent care for chest pain lasting more than 15 minutes unrelieved by rest or nitroglycerin, especially with dyspnea or diaphoresis.
