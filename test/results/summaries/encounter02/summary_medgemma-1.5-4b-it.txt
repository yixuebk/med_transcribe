Subjective:
Patient reports intermittent substernal pressure-like chest discomfort over three to four months, triggered by exertion (climbing stairs, brisk walking) and sometimes at rest. The pain radiates to the left shoulder, medial aspect of the left arm, and occasionally the jaw. Episodes last five to ten minutes and resolve with rest or sublingual nitroglycerin. Associated symptoms during episodes include dyspnea on exertion, diaphoresis, mild nausea, and two instances of near syncope without loss of consciousness. Patient also reports orthopnea (needing two pillows to sleep) and paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnea (waking up gasping for air) over the last month. Additionally, patient notes bilateral ankle swelling with sock indentation marks and a weight gain of approximately eight pounds over two months.

Objective:
Patient's home blood pressure is typically 145/90 mmHg. Current examination reveals BP 152/94 mmHg, heart rate 92 bpm (irregular), possible S3 gallop, soft systolic murmur at the apex, bibasilar crackles, and 1+ pitting edema bilaterally in ankles. Laboratory studies ordered include 12-lead ECG, lipid panel, comprehensive metabolic panel (CMP), BNP, repeat hemoglobin A1C (8.2% reported previously). Transthoracic echocardiogram (TTE) is ordered to assess left ventricular ejection fraction (LVEF), regional wall motion abnormalities, and valvular function. Stress test with nuclear imaging is also ordered due to intermediate-to-high pretest probability for significant coronary artery disease (CAD).

Assessment:
Patient presents with symptoms highly suggestive of angina pectoris (exertional chest pressure radiating to left arm/jaw, relieved by rest/nitroglycerin) and potential myocardial ischemia (dyspnea on exertion, diaphoresis, presyncope). The presence of orthopnea, paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnea, bilateral ankle edema, weight gain, bibasilar crackles, irregular heart rate, possible S3 gallop, and apical systolic murmur are concerning for developing congestive heart failure (CHF), potentially secondary to ischemic cardiomyopathy. Patient has multiple cardiovascular risk factors including long-standing hypertension (BP 145/90 home), type 2 diabetes mellitus (HbA1C 8.2%), hyperlipidemia, prior significant tobacco use, and a strong family history of premature CAD and CHF. The clinical picture points towards obstructive coronary artery disease with possible ischemic cardiomyopathy and early CHF. Further investigation is warranted.

Plan:
Ordered investigations: 12-lead ECG, lipid panel, CMP, BNP, repeat HbA1C (8.2%), TTE (LVEF, regional wall motion abnormalities, valvular function), stress test with nuclear imaging. Medical therapy optimization: Up-titrate ACE inhibitor or consider ARNI if systolic dysfunction confirmed; initiate beta blocker; add loop diuretic for symptomatic volume overload; ensure high-intensity statin use; consider SGLT2 inhibitor given diabetes and potential CHF; implement sodium restriction (< 2g/day). Patient education: Strict instructions to seek emergent care for chest pain lasting >15 minutes, unrelieved by rest or nitroglycerin, especially with severe dyspnea or diaphoresis. Follow-up scheduled.
