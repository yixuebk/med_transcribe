Subjective:
53-year-old male with history of hypertension presenting for routine follow-up reporting new onset headaches over the past three weeks. Headaches are described as dull, bifrontal, about four to five out of ten in intensity, occur mostly in the late afternoon, no associated photophobia, no phonophobia, no nausea or vomiting, denies visual changes, no focal neurologic deficits reported, no recent head trauma. He does report increased work stress and poor sleep, about four to five hours per night.

Objective:
Blood pressure today is elevated at 156 over 94. Repeat blood pressure reading on manual recheck was 158 over 96. Heart rate 78, afebrile, BMI 31.2. On exam, neurologic exam is non-focal, cranial nerves 2 through 12 intact, strength 5 out of 5 in all extremities, sensation intact, fundoscopic exam, limited but no obvious papilledema, cardiovascular exam, regular rate and rhythm, no murmurs. He states he has been intermittently missing doses of his medication about two to three times per week.

Assessment:
Poorly controlled hypertension, likely secondary to medication non-adherence with tension-type headaches versus hypertension-related headaches. No red flag symptoms at this time.

Plan:
Reinforce medication compliance, continue amlodipine 10 milligrams daily and add lisinopril 10 milligrams daily. Order basic metabolic panel today for baseline renal function. Recommend sleep hygiene measures and stress reduction. Advise patient on warning signs, worsening headache, visual changes, neurologic deficits to go to the emergency department if those occur. Follow up in four weeks for blood pressure recheck.
