Subjective:
53-year-old male presenting for routine follow-up of hypertension with new onset headaches reported over the past three weeks. Patient reports dull, bifrontal headaches rated 4-5/10 in intensity occurring primarily in the late afternoon. No photophobia, phonophobia, nausea, vomiting, visual changes, or focal neurologic deficits. He denies recent head trauma. Patient also reports increased work stress and poor sleep (4-5 hours per night) and intermittent missed doses of amlodipine (2-3 times/week).

Objective:
Vitals: BP 156/94 (158/96 on manual recheck), HR 78, afebrile, BMI 31.2. Neurological exam: Non-focal, cranial nerves II-XII intact, strength 5/5 bilaterally, sensation intact. Fundoscopic exam: Limited but no obvious papilledema. Cardiovascular exam: Regular rate and rhythm, no murmurs.

Assessment:
Poorly controlled hypertension likely secondary to medication non-adherence with tension-type headaches versus hypertension-related headaches. No red flag symptoms at this time.

Plan:
Reinforce medication compliance. Continue amlodipine 10 mg daily and add lisinopril 10 mg daily. Order basic metabolic panel today for baseline renal function. Recommend sleep hygiene measures and stress reduction techniques. Advise patient on warning signs (worsening headache, visual changes, neurologic deficits) to prompt emergency department presentation.
