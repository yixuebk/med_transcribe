Subjective:
53-year-old male with a history of hypertension, currently taking amlodipine 10 mg daily. Presents for routine follow-up reporting new onset headaches over the past three weeks. Headaches are described as dull, bifrontal, rated 4/10 intensity, occurring mostly in the late afternoon. No associated photophobia, phonophobia, nausea, or vomiting. Denies visual changes and focal neurologic deficits. Reports increased work stress and poor sleep (4-5 hours per night). Patient admits to intermittent medication non-adherence (missing doses 2-3 times/week). Blood pressure measured at 158/96 mmHg on manual recheck.

Objective:
Vital signs: BP 158/96 mmHg, HR 78 bpm, RR 16 bpm, Temp 37.0°C (afebrile), BMI 31.2 kg/m². Physical exam: Neurologic exam is non-focal; cranial nerves II-XII intact, strength 5/5 in all extremities, sensation intact. Fundoscopic exam limited but no obvious papilledema. Cardiovascular exam reveals a regular rate and rhythm with no murmurs. Laboratory: Basic metabolic panel ordered.

Assessment:
Poorly controlled hypertension (BP 158/96 mmHg) likely secondary to medication non-adherence. Tension-type headache versus hypertension-related headache. No red flag symptoms identified at this time.

Plan:
1. Reinforce medication compliance with amlodipine 10 mg daily and lisinopril 10 mg daily. 2. Order basic metabolic panel for baseline renal function. 3. Recommend sleep hygiene measures and stress reduction techniques. 4. Advise patient on warning signs requiring emergency department evaluation (worsening headache, visual changes, neurologic deficits). 5. Follow up in four weeks for blood pressure recheck.
