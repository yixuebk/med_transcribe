Subjective:
53-year-old male with a history of hypertension currently on amlodipine 10 mg daily. Presents for routine follow-up and reports new onset headaches for the past three weeks, described as dull and bifrontal, with an intensity of 4-5 out of 10 occurring mostly in late afternoon. No associated photophobia, phonophobia, nausea, or vomiting. Denies visual changes and focal neurologic deficits. Reports increased work stress and poor sleep (4-5 hours per night).

Objective:
Vital signs: BP 158/96, Heart rate 78, Afebrile, BMI 31.2. Neurologic exam non-focal, cranial nerves 2-12 intact, strength 5/5 in all extremities, sensation intact. Fundoscopic exam limited but no obvious papilledema. Cardiovascular exam shows regular rate and rhythm, no murmurs. Patient reports missing doses of medication 2-3 times per week.

Assessment:
Poorly controlled hypertension, likely secondary to medication non-adherence. Tension-type headaches versus hypertension-related headaches. No red flag symptoms present.

Plan:
Reinforce medication compliance. Continue amlodipine 10 mg daily and add lisinopril 10 mg daily. Order basic metabolic panel for renal function baseline. Recommend sleep hygiene measures and stress reduction techniques. Advise on warning signs (worsening headache, visual changes, neurological deficits) that necessitate immediate emergency care. Follow-up in four weeks for blood pressure recheck.
